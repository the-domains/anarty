I make art because I love doing it. It is the house where my soul lives, a window into my subconscious, a door my ideas leap out of into the wider world.

I was educated as both a librarian, and a teacher, and developed a host of skills relevant to my working life. Offering information and teaching others came quite naturally. But I always made art. Teaching art to adults is more of a collaboration than with children, because in parentis locus no longer applies. I find that collaboration hugely satisfying. I would say my specialty is encouraging creativity.

On the other hand, I quite happily lose my self in my studio, where I can revel in satisfying my own whims and curiosity. I am very curious about ideas, concepts, mysteries. Art, along with poetry and reading, offer paths to pursue them, and inspire methods for expressing them.

The greatest lottery I could ever win is Enlightenment. It is the only lottery I invest in.
